## Recommended Path

![My recommended path, if I had to get certified from zero.](/recommended-path.png)

1. **Pick an associate level certification in one of the clouds**: This depends on the market where you're at. For example, Azure is more used in EU. AWS is globally used. But for the Saudi market, GCP, OCI, and Alibaba Cloud are leaders.
   - Why not start at the foundation level? Foundation level certifications are targeting business-oriented people. It's not worth bothering with them for tech-adjacent crowds (like yourselves, probably)

2. **Red Hat Certified System Administrator (RHCSA)**: The king of Linux certifications. Linux is all over the cloud, and it will not change. If you are unfamiliar with Linux, this is the best certificate you can study for that will get you ready for managing Linux servers and systems.
   - Do NOT bother with the [Linux Foundation Certified System Administrator (LFCS)](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/linux-foundation-certified-sysadmin-lfcs/)

3. **Networking:**

Choose one:

a. Cisco Certified Networking Associate: More networking knowledge than you need for the Cloud, but so helpful. Opens up more doors for you besides cloud - especially with RHCSA.

b. CompTIA Network+: The lightweight networking certificate. Contains just what you need to know how to design your network cloud resources.

4. **Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA)**: Just like Linux, Kubernetes is everywhere, especially in Saudi Arabia. Even though you'll probably work with managed Kubernetes services like Google Kubernetes Engine (GKE), it's insanely useful to know the inner workings of Kubernetes components to get good at debugging its issues.

5. **STOP!** Choose the "Professional" level cloud architecting certification **FOR THE CLOUD YOU WORK ON!** Do not bother getting a professional certification while unemployed, it will probably work against you!

## Certifications are not enough

Certifications _mostly_ prove you know the theory _somewhat_. You know what proves you know the theory and the application of it? **PROJECTS.**

If you are in the tech industry, you have to have side projects. It does not matter if your project solves world hunger or is just a personal website. Just the fact that you are PRACTICING the technologies you will work on gives you an **INSANE** advantage ahead of others who are just cert-collecting.

1. Do projects.

2. Document your findings/process.

3. Publish it somewhere where people can see your projects.

4. Repeat.

## Publishing Projects on-the-cheap

So, if you're building a cloud projects you're probably scared of running over a certain budget of costs. A simple way to test and document your project is to choose "Pay-as-you-go" payment schemes and not subscriptions. Most cloud providers offer both payment types.

1. Build your project.

2. Publish your project online.

3. Record a demo, whether a silent quick demo or with voice-overs, it doesn't matter. Just explain your project, and go through its main functions.

4. Once your demo is recorded, take down the project and its resources.

Depending on how long you spend recording the demo, you will pay literally CENTS only. And you never have to be more.
